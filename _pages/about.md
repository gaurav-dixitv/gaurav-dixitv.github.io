I am a postdoctoral researcher at the [AI-Caring Institute](https://ai-caring.org/) and the [Collaborative Robotics and Intelligent Systems (CoRIS) Institute](https://engineering.oregonstate.edu/research/CoRIS) at [Oregon State University](https://engineering.oregonstate.edu/academics/programs/robotics). I completed my PhD at the [ Autonomous Agents and Distributed Intelligence Lab](https://web.engr.oregonstate.edu/~ktumer/) with [Dr. Kagan Tumer](https://web.engr.oregonstate.edu/~ktumer/), where I developed learning and diversity-search techniques for cooperative multi-agent settings.

My research is at the intersection of reinforcement learning, evolutionary algorithms, game theory, and ethics. Broadly, I am interested in developing methods that allow asymmetric agents—agents with distinct capabilities and objectives—to cooperate and work in teams with diverse partners.

At the AI-Caring Institute, my work synthesizes multi-objective solution concepts that facilitate collective reasoning required to pursue high-level, long-term, dynamic, and potentially conflicting objectives such as an older adult’s independence and safety. This induces three distinct challenges: 1) Identifying the representative set of care coordination tasks, such as collaborative cleaning and health monitoring, that encapsulate the diverse responsibilities and interactions necessary for providing comprehensive care in a healthcare setting; 2) Inferring the core characteristics of the tasks such as the required agent capabilities, the associated objectives, tasks priorities and interdependence, and the expected cooperative outcomes to ensure wellbeing; and 3) Developing a coverage set of diverse trade-offs that account for the collective considerations of caregivers, healthcare provides and embodied agents.
